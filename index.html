Biography

Steven Paul "Steve" Jobs was an American information technology entrepreneur and inventor. He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc; CEO and majority shareholder of Pixar Animation Studios; a member of The Walt Disney Company's board of directors following its acquisition of Pixar; and founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT Inc.

Jobs is widely recognized as a pioneer of the microcomputer revolution of the 1970s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak. Jobs also helped to popularize the personal computer with the introduction of the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers.

In the late 1970s, Jobs, along with Apple's marketing chief Mike Markkula and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak, designed, developed, and marketed one of the first commercially successful lines of personal computers, the Apple II series.

Revolutionary Products

Apple II

One of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers, launched in 1977, that helped establish the personal computer market.

Macintosh

Revolutionary personal computer with graphical user interface that changed how people interact with computers forever.

iPod

Portable media player that transformed the music industry and made digital music mainstream.

iPhone

Revolutionary smartphone that redefined mobile communication and created the modern smartphone era.

iPad

Tablet computer that created an entirely new product category and changed how we consume digital content.

Pixar

Animation studio that revolutionized filmmaking with computer-generated imagery, starting with Toy Story.

Legacy

Steve Jobs left an indelible mark on technology, design, and popular culture. His relentless pursuit of perfection and innovative thinking transformed multiple industries - from personal computing and animated movies to music, phones, and tablet computing.

His philosophy of simplicity and user-centered design continues to influence product development across the world. Jobs didn't just create products; he created experiences that people love and use every day.

More than a decade after his passing, his vision of technology that "just works" remains the gold standard for user experience design.
